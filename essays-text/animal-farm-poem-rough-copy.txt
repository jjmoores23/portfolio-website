Untitled
Jacob Moores
2021
Laden with labor, weight of the farm on the back of the mammal
Years go by, wait for life to pass to blow out your final candle
Majors dying sermon, All Animals are equal
Change your lives for the better, overrun the People
Animalism is established, The rebellion is plotted
Midsummer's eve, Animals guts are rotted
In blood shed, lashes were handed
Through marks of biters mandibles, legs were branded
Burn the Regalia of slavery, and look upon your new kingdom
Commence in Commandments, Rules to revere until cometh thy kingdom *
In* comes the harvest, with sews greater than ever seen
No waste, no haste, a taste of a life which is pristine
Raise your flag, sing your anthem, rinse and repeat
Learn your alphabet, master the arts, remnants of humans post gnashing of teeth
Through Sunday lies the day of rest, although work is still to be done
Homogenized society shows imparity, dairy distributed to mammals whom minds run *
Fast forward to better days, pigeons foretell of a non-salem battle*
Replicate Caesar, show a rout but upon man prepare to rattle
Flank the rear, derrieres is where the humans are to be dismantled
The battle was won, but at the cost of mental scars, eyes glazed over like enamel
Onwards and upwards, a windmill is to be built!
A brighter future for all, but looming is conflict still
Snowball versus Napoleon, confliction of ideas lead to animosity
Backstab of biblical proportions, animals flock to witness the atrocity
Out is Snowball thrown, and on his high horse Napoleon supersedes
March forth to the windmill*, hard work for all, and to all godspeed *
Windmill built up, windmill plummets down
Presumed vehemence of Snowball, remnants scattered round
Ambiguous to most, Animalism (is) to tumble like previous trestles
Yearning for more than sovereignty, the pigs bond like cresol
Changes in the rules, changes in equality
Soon to follow suit, an attenuation in society
Power hungry Napoleon, tailgated by absent minded theists
Pray you never squealed, lest you become a scrap in the mound of miscreants
New threat to ensue, a fight of unbalance that in terror is twofold
Evisceration of the Windmill will do, worse than rubbing out the de trop quadruped mold
Battle won in the eyes of the blind, more rebuilding to come
Speeches ringing in the ears of the deaf, the swine still have worlds to run
Nature causes’ avunculicide, Boxer can work hard no longer
Pale Horse drives comrade away, Boxers resting place the farm can only ponder


One gladiator on fours leaves, and on two the pigs learn to walk
To commemorate the occasion, enter humans to swindle, schmooze and mock
The outcome of this interaction, nobody knows for certain
All they know is manor, and work until their flatline closes the curtains
